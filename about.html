The Story / कहानी

A Mission Born from Pain, Built on Science

पिता की बीमारी से मिली सीख — मिट्टी को बचाने की ठानी

Dr. Sharvan Kumar Yadav was born into a farming family in Rajasthan. Always passionate about agriculture, he pursued higher education — earning his MSc in Organic Farming from Karnataka with an ICAR JRF scholarship in 2012, followed by his Ph.D. in Agronomy with specialisation in Organic Farming from Maharana Pratap University of Agriculture & Technology (MPUAT), Udaipur.

The turning point came when his father — a healthy farmer with no bad habits — was diagnosed with cancer. "We realised chemical food was silently poisoning us," Dr. Sharvan recalls. This moment sparked a lifelong commitment to organic farming.

After completing his Ph.D., he worked as a Senior Research Fellow at ICAR for two years. During the 2020 lockdown, he came home and started with just 17 vermicompost beds. Today, the farm at Sundarpura runs 2500–2800 beds on 5 hectares, producing 1500 tonnes of vermicompost per year — one of the largest in India.

"My goal was to give good organic manure to farmers across the country, so that more and more people join organic farming."

— Dr. Sharvan Kumar Yadav

ICAR Recognition / ICAR मान्यता

Published as a Model Farmer Innovator by ICAR

भारतीय कृषि अनुसंधान परिषद द्वारा आदर्श नवाचारी किसान के रूप में प्रकाशित

Dr. Sharvan Kumar Yadav's farm and innovation work was featured by ICAR (Indian Council of Agricultural Research) as a model startup story — recognising his contributions to vermicompost production, organic input development, and farmer training.

His work includes developing multiple vermicompost production systems (above-ground, underground, flour-based, HDPE, windrow), creating organic formulations like Brahmastra, Ghanjeevamrit, Dashparni Ark, and enriched vermicompost pellets.

ICAR institutes, state universities, and ATMA officials have validated his innovations through farm visits, research interactions, and official certifications.
